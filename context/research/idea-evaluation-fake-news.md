# Idea Evaluation — Fake News Detection Startup

**Score: 7.5/10. Strong idea, real risks, clear path to revenue.**

## Feature 1: Viral Prediction
**Verdict: High potential. Hard to build. Genuinely differentiated.**

Academic foundations solid (Max Planck Curb algorithm, Albany Comment Storm Severity). Neither commercialised. Core challenge is data access, not the model. X Pro API: $5,000/month. Third-party providers (SociaVault/Xpoz): $200-500/month. B2B newsroom is the buyer — a "high viral risk + low credibility" flag within 30 minutes is worth paying for.

Risk: False positives. The model needs confidence scores, not binary verdicts.

## Feature 2: Social Tagging (@unfaked bot)
**Verdict: Strong consumer hook. Technically feasible. Precedent exists.**

Community Notes is the analogue — slow (hours), crowdsourced, platform-locked. The Unfaked bot responds to a tag within seconds with a structured verdict. ClaimBuster (UT Arlington) proved the architecture. Key decisions: speed (<30s to feel useful) and tone (not preachy).

Platform risk: X API terms prohibit competing with X's own features.

## Feature 3: Poster Identity Detection
**Verdict: Technically feasible. Legally dangerous. Needs careful scoping.**

GDPR prohibits processing geolocation without consent. FTC banned four data brokers in 2024 for selling precise location data. The right build: private intelligence layer (internal credibility score only), never public-facing identity output.

## Recommended Priorities
1. Build Feature 2 (bot) first — fastest to market, builds training data
2. Build Feature 1 (viral prediction) second — B2B newsroom product  
3. Build Feature 3 last and carefully — internal credibility layer only
