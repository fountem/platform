# Idea Evaluation — Voter Intelligence Platform

**Viability Score: 8/10 — stronger commercial idea than standalone fake news detection.**

## The Core Proposition
Enter location before election → see every candidate on ballot → for each: where they actually stand, whether past actions match stated positions, who funded them, whether specific claims they've made are true.

## The Gap
Every existing voter tool answers one of two questions: *what does this party say?* or *did this politician lie?* Fountem answers a third: *which party's policies actually address the problem you care about?*

## Competitive Landscape
- ISideWith — quiz-based, relies on candidate self-reporting, no evidence layer
- VoteSmart — rigorous US tool, poor UX, no AI synthesis
- BallotReady — local US races, no fact-checking
- Electoral Commission — primary data, not consumer product
- TheyWorkForYou — MPs only, no synthesis

## The Honest Risks
1. **Perceived bias** — existential risk. Solution: visible citations on everything, separate data from interpretation, public advisory board.
2. **Defamation exposure** — manageable with rigorous sourcing. Truth and public interest defences available UK law.
3. **Data coverage** — UK parliamentary candidates rich, local council candidates variable.

## Business Model
- **B2C subscription:** £5-10/month premium tier. At 0.5% of UK registered voters (200k) paying £5/month = £1M ARR.
- **B2B licensing:** API access to newsrooms/civic orgs. £500-2,000/month per organisation.
- **Grant funding:** bridge to product-market fit. Nuffield, JRRT, Omidyar Network, Knight Foundation.
- **Advertising:** avoid entirely. Conflict of interest kills brand trust.

## Recommended Build
Start with UK parliamentary candidates, single election cycle (May 2026). Four data pillars: positions, voting record, donors, fact-checks.
