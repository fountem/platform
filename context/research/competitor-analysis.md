# Competitor Analysis — Fountem

**Fountem operates in two adjacent markets where the existing tools are either built for institutions, not people, or built for people but without the evidence layer that makes them trustworthy.** No competitor in the UK — and none globally — has combined policy effectiveness intelligence with misinformation detection under a single consumer-facing brand. That gap is the opportunity.

## Summary Gap

Three gaps exist that no current player occupies:

1. **Policy effectiveness analysis for consumers.** Every voter tool in the UK presents what parties say. None evaluate whether what parties say is causally connected to the problems voters actually have. The root cause layer is entirely unbuilt.

2. **Consumer-facing misinformation detection in the UK.** Full Fact operates on a 24–48 hour editorial cycle and checks roughly 2 claims per day. A tool that can respond in minutes to a tagged post or flagged video is structurally absent from the UK market.

3. **Viral prediction.** Every detection product is reactive. The Max Planck Institute's Curb algorithm exists academically. No company has commercialised it.

## Key Competitor Notes

**Reality Defender** — Most capable full-stack detection, enterprise-only, no public interface, no social tagging, pricing undisclosed.

**Logically** — £19M raised, monitors 120M+ domains, B2B/gov intelligence, not consumer. Has UK TikTok/Facebook fact-checking partnerships. Cannot publish methodology due to government client relationships.

**Full Fact** — UK's leading fact-checker, IFCN certified. Checks ~750 claims/year. Potential partner, not competitor. 24-48h editorial cycle.

**Penemue (Germany)** — Raised €1.7M on 3 April 2026. 89 languages, hate speech moderation for institutions. Closest European analog but different wedge (moderation vs fact-checking).

## Competitive Moat

The structural defence against platform threats and enterprise competitors extending downmarket is the same: brand trust built in public, over time, through a transparent methodology that platforms and enterprise tools will never be able to claim.
