# Content Complaints, Right of Reply & Takedown Policy — Fountem / Unfaked

**Last updated:** June 17, 2026 · **Status:** Operational policy (draft) · **Not legal advice.**

This is the internal operating procedure behind the public "challenge a verdict / report a
problem" routes. A fast, fair, documented process here is **both** the ethical default **and**
a core element of our Defamation Act 2013 s.4 public-interest defence and our UK GDPR
data-subject-rights obligations.

---

## 1. Channels

- **Email:** `corrections@fountem.ai` / `legal@unfaked.ai` (route both to a monitored inbox).
- **Per-verdict link:** every verdict card and archive entry shows a "**Challenge this
  verdict / report a problem**" link.
- **Data-rights:** `privacy@` for access/rectification/erasure (UK GDPR).

## 2. What people can ask for

1. **Right of reply** — the subject of a verdict provides context/evidence we should consider.
2. **Correction** — they say the verdict is wrong (factually or in its detection signals).
3. **Erasure / removal** — they ask us to take down an archive item (UK GDPR erasure or
   harm-based removal).
4. **De-identification** — keep the analysis but remove personal identifiers.

## 3. Triage & SLA

| Step | Target |
|---|---|
| Acknowledge receipt | ≤ **2 working days** |
| Initial assessment + risk triage | ≤ **5 working days** |
| Resolution (correct / uphold / remove / reply published) | ≤ **10 working days** |
| **Urgent** (clear serious harm, election-period, plausible false positive about a named person) | **Same/next working day**: provisionally **unpublish** pending review |

## 4. Decision process

1. **Log** the request (date, item, requester, nature) in the corrections log.
2. **Provisional unpublish** if urgent / plausible serious harm.
3. **Re-review** the verdict: re-run signals / re-check sources; route to **human review**.
4. **Decide**: uphold, correct, add right-of-reply note, de-identify, or remove.
5. **Record the outcome** and reasoning. **Never silently edit** an archived verdict — mark
   it **"corrected"/"updated"** with a date and (where appropriate) what changed. Preserve an
   internal audit trail even if the public item is removed.
6. **Respond** to the requester explaining the decision and how to escalate (ICO for data
   matters; we cannot give them legal advice).

## 5. Erasure / removal assessment (UK GDPR)

Balance the **public interest** in keeping a public-figure verdict against the individual's
rights. Lean toward **removal/de-identification** where: the subject is a **private
individual**; the basis was weak; the harm is real and current; or the verdict has been
**corrected to no longer support** the original claim. Lean toward **retention** where the
subject is a **public figure**, the matter is of **ongoing public interest**, and the verdict
is **well-evidenced** — but **always** offer a right-of-reply annotation.

## 6. Versioning & transparency

- Every published verdict has a **version** and a **last-updated** date.
- Material changes create a **new version**; the change is noted (relevant to the single-
  publication rule — see defamation memo).
- Maintain a **public corrections log** (a `/corrections` page is recommended) for trust.

## 7. Records

Keep, for at least the limitation period, an internal record of: the original verdict and its
basis, the complaint, the re-review, the decision and reasoning, and all correspondence. This
is the evidence of our "responsible publication" process.

## 8. Escalation

- **Data matters:** tell complainants they may complain to the **ICO**.
- **Defamation/legal threats:** acknowledge, do **not** admit liability, preserve records,
  and **escalate to counsel** immediately.
