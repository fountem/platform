# Product Strategy Memo — Why Fountem's Feature Roadmap Creates Moat

**Audience:** investors, grant evaluators, strategic partners

## The Short Answer
Fountem's roadmap is not disconnected features. It is a sequence of systems that reinforce one another. Three strategic assets compound over time: **structured evidence**, **distribution efficiency**, and **institutional usefulness**.

## The Market Problem
Voter-information products help people discover candidates but rarely explain whether the stories voters hear about their problems are true. Fact-checking products correct claims but don't connect corrections back to policy choices. Verification platforms are B2B workflow tools. None connect: problem voter cares about → evidence on causation → claims circulating about it → policies that do/don't address it.

## The Three Strategic Assets

### 1. Structured Evidence
Most products link to evidence. Fountem builds structured relationships between concerns, claims, sources, root causes, and policy responses. This is more defensible than content volume — a competitor can publish a similar verdict, but replicating a navigable evidence chain with verified relationships is hard.

### 2. Distribution Efficiency
Misinformation wins because it's easy to share. Correction Packs and Claim-to-Policy links make truth competitive in the attention economy — a correction as easy to forward as the original claim.

### 3. Institutional Usefulness
The same system that helps a voter understand a claim can help a journalist verify it or a civic org explain it. Dual usefulness creates multiple adoption routes and multiple revenue routes.

## Why Sequencing Matters
Trust-facing features first → distribution features second → workflow features third.
This lowers technical risk, product risk, and business risk simultaneously.

## Why This Beats Generic AI Fact-Checking
Generic AI fact-checking is becoming crowded. Fountem wins as *trust infrastructure* — a product that serves voters, journalists, and partners — not as another fact-checking bot.
