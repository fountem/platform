# Brand Analysis — Fountem

## Verdict
The name Fountem is a placeholder name while branding is finalised. It needs deliberate work on social shareability and working-class resonance.

## What Works
- Derives from *fount* — the source, the origin, the place truth comes from
- Politically neutral by design (no partisan colour associations)
- Domain package is strong: fountem.com, fountem.co.uk, fountem.ai all available
- Works as a calm anchor for sharp content

## What Doesn't
- Invented word requires learning on first encounter
- Does not carry kinetic energy — the content earns shares, not the name
- May not immediately resonate with Reform voter demographic (35+ male, routine occupation, sub-£20k income)

## The Bot: Unfaked (unfaked.ai)
Score: 9/10. Direct, punchy, anti-establishment energy. The .ai TLD signals AI mechanism. Short and works as a social handle. "This just got unfaked" is a sentence that already exists in the culture.

## Bot Reply Format
> **@unfaked_ai** `unfaked.ai`
> ⚠️ Misleading — [one sentence]
> Source: [Organisation, date]
> `unfaked.ai/check/[id]`

## What Makes It Spread
Not the name — the verdicts. A Fountem post that says "Reform claims X. The ONS data says something different. Here's what it actually shows." gets shared not because of the name but because it gives the reader something they feel empowered to pass on.
