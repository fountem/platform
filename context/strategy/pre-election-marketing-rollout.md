# Fountem — Pre-Election Marketing & Brand Rollout

**Sprint window:** 27 March – 1 May 2026 (5 weeks)
**Tagline:** "AI-generated images of local election candidates are already circulating in the UK. Fountem is the tool that catches them."

## Launch Hook: Wakefield Deepfake
Wakefield councillor Armaan Khan had his image doctored by AI — placed next to a housing estate he'd actively opposed. Image circulated before May 2026 elections. BBC covered it. No fast correction mechanism existed. This is the product story.

## Channel Strategy

| Channel | Play |
|---|---|
| X (@unfaked_ai) | Bot + organic. 50 verdicts by election day, zero public corrections. |
| WhatsApp | Correction Packs — 5 cards live by end of Week 1. |
| Facebook groups | Be useful before being present. Correct claims without branding, then link when asked. |
| Local press | Yorkshire Post, MEN, Birmingham Mail, Liverpool Echo, The Argus. One paragraph + Wakefield case + standing offer to check claims. |
| Reddit | r/unitedkingdom + local subs. Same pattern: correct first, link second. |
| Email | Simple signup on fountem.ai. Target: 500 subscribers by election day. |

## Partnership Targets (in priority order)
1. **Democracy Club** — attribution link + methodology mention. Easy ask.
2. **Full Fact** — methodology review conversation. Not endorsement. Week 3.
3. **mySociety** — co-authored blog post "AI, deepfakes, and May 2026 elections." Week 3.
4. **Bureau of Investigative Journalism** — tip about Wakefield + overseas content farms angle.
5. **National Association of Councillors** — Correction Pack templates for affected councillors.

## Success Metrics
- 50 verdicts published by election day (quality > volume)
- Zero public corrections to any verdict
- 5 verdicts cited by journalist or civic org
- 500 email subscribers
- 10,000 unique visitors to fountem.ai
- 20 Correction Packs shared 100+ times each
- @unfaked_ai tagged organically by someone Fountem didn't ask — the key signal
